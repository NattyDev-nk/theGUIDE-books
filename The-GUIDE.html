Inside the Book...

A quick glance at the topics you'll learn

The best way to get high grades on an exam is to consider the entire length of your course as a pre-exam, preparation period. Because courses can last a few months to a whole year, you may find it difficult to make the connection between your daily homework, periodic assignments, and your final exam. To get high marks, start by recognizing that all academic work done through the semester, summer, or school year is exam studying. This chapter will give you strategies of achieving academic excellence which includes setting your goals, managing your time......

In order to do well on an exam, you spend hours, days or even months studying. This preparation can be more effective if you know your personal learning style. Your learning style is simply the way you learn best. It is as unique as you are. Understanding your learning style can help you remember information more easily, solve problems more effectively, and understand concepts more naturally.

Are you intelligent? Are your friends intelligent? Are your teachers or professors intelligent? Your immediate answer to these questions should be, Yes!. Today, the idea of intelligence is no longer limited to only one kind of intelligence, popularly known as IQ, or Intelligence Quotient. Today./'s view has expanded to include many different kinds of intelligence, which cover a wide variety of strengths. According to the theory of Multiple Intelligences, proposed by Dr. Howard Gardiner of Harvard University, we have not one, but seven or eight different intelligences or ways in which we express our intelligence.
In this chapter, you will learn about linguistic intelligence, Mathematical/Logic Intelligence, Music Intelligence...

To earn high marks on a test, you must do three things: study the material, find out what kind of test it is, and learn effective test taking strategies. Some strategies are general and apply to all tests while others will help you with specific types of questions.
In this chapter you will learn about general and specific test-taking stragies

One of the most frequent and reasonable questions that students ask is: What should I be doing to get a better grade? Of course, the answer to this question will depend on a number of factors. For example, what is required of an essay answer will clearly vary according to the precise question set. Equally, the standard expected of essay writing is likely to be higher on more advanced undergraduate courses than on those at entry level. In this chapter, you will learn about structure of marking scheme......

Before looking at the course description and past question in the subsequent part of this book it is necessary for us to know the guiding principles and theoretical rationale for deciding on what materials and methods to use. This chapter examines the ASSURE model developed by Heinch R., Molenda M. and Russel James (1985) in their book Instructional media and the new Technology of Instruction which was modified to fit to the context of this book.

The problems solved in this chapter will give ideas on how to solve applied mechaics issues relating to force and moment reactions of a tree trunk base, the least force required to move a rectangular block, the magnitude of the couple that must be applied to the sleeve in order to draw the rods closer together, explain the principle of transmissibility, laws that govern solid friction, the varignon's theory....

The problems solved in this chapter will give ideas on how to solve Engineering Thermodynaics issues relating to Laws of Thermodynamics, T-S Diagrams, change in entropy, heat supplied, work done, thermal efficiency of Otto cycle, compression ratio, adiabatic processes, polytropic processes ....
